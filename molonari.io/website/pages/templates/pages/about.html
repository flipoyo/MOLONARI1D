About MOLONARI

Monitoring local stream-aquifer exchanges since 2021

What Is MOLONARI?

MOLONARI stands for "monitoring local des échanges nappe-rivière", which translates to local monitoring of stream-aquifer exchanges (LOMOS).

The project develops a comprehensive environmental monitoring solution for water and heat exchanges in riverbed environments. A MOLONARI1D device provides temperature and pressure sensors in the riverbed at depths ranging from 0 to approximately 40 cm. Over time, these measurements reveal information about water and heat exchanges between the river and the underlying aquifer, as well as the aquifer's physical properties (porosity, permeability, etc.).

Key Principles

Low-Tech Production

Devices can be assembled with readily available components in fablabs and university workshops.

Open Source & Replicability

All hardware designs, firmware and software are open source and documented for replication.

Scalability

From a single sensor to networks of hundreds of devices across large river systems.

Technology Readiness

Current TRL: 5–6 — Laboratory validation complete, field deployment in progress. The system has been successfully tested underwater in laboratory conditions and is transitioning to operational field environments.

Contributors

MOLONARI1D is a teaching and research project held at Mines Paris – PSL since 2021, under the supervision of Nicolas Flipo, Aurélien Baudin, Agnès Rivière, Thomas Romary and Fabien Ors.

The project is integrated into the French research project EQUIPEX+ TERRA FORMA led by CNRS.
